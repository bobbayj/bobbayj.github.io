About me

Quick Intro

Born and raised in Sydney, I am a young and hungry professional. I want to do so much, but I only have so much time to commit. So, I put together this website for a little bit of soul-searching, as well as to share myself with the world.

Here's a quick summary about me:

  • I am always down for a challenge and to learn new skills, cultures, and facts
  • I am passionate about raising the standard of healthcare
  • I believe using AI and machine learning can significantly improve efficiency and deliver better outcomes in all industries (including healthcare)
  • I believe Elon Musk is actually going to reach Mars within my lifetime
  • I enjoy building things that deliver a smile to the end-user
  • I enjoy investing - time and/or money - in world-changing, purpose-driven companies
  • I strive to be a technologist - my python-fu gets better everyday
  • I want to travel to every country in the world

    • I am an investor

    I feel fortunate having studied Finance at the University of Sydney. It taught me that the expectations around money drives almost all decisions in our capitalist society. While that may sound negative, I think it is important as it provides the proper incentive mechanisms to reward the innovators, entrepeneurs, and change-makers.

    While I am on my own entrepreneurial journey, I have invested a substantial amount of my savings into the Australian stock market. While a lot of the companies I've invested in are a part of the healthcare industry, I also believe it is important to support the next Google's and Facebook's of the world. Hence, you will often see me reading the news with a commercial lens; information moves markets!

    I aim to be a technologist

    We have lived through massive technological advancements, and we have seen how technology can positively improve our standard of living. I want to be part of this change, and to do so, I need to be well-versed in the language that technology uses to communicate.

    I have been developing my understanding of programming ever since I was introduced to Python in my second year of univeristy. My ability to breakdown complex concepts into bite-sized, simple to understand pieces helped me land a position as a Tutor for ENGG1801: Engineering Computing. Yet, teaching Excel and Python functions and concepts doesn't really prepare you for the real world of software development.

    Since then, I have continued to expand my capability in both programming and data science. I've wrangled with some large datasets at a Finance internship, using SQL, built dashboards to visualise complex datasets in PowerBI, and have prototyped web-apps using the Vue.js paradigm. Most notably, I centred my Honours thesis around deep learning, using LSTMs and RNNs to see if there is any predictability from limit-order book information on the stock market.

    While I am comfortable using git, I am beginning to take my development capability to another level. Diving into DevOps, I am learning about the DevOps lifecycle - with a focus on CI / CD - and familiarising myself with Docker (because maintaining environments couldn't be made easier). On the analytical side, I am seeking more projects that will involve the use of large datasets so I can dive into Big Data paradigms like Scala and Hadoop. And, of course, the ultimate goal is to be able to launch AI-powered systems with state-of-the-art machine learning algorithms.

    By the way, make sure you check out my projects on Github!

    I am Commercial at heart

    But, my greatest fear as a technologist is becoming a 'code monkey'. I believe I have more value as a decision-maker, problem solver, and innovator. Not only do I want to make great technology, I want to make useful, beautiful technology for the end-user. Hence, I find it difficult to leave behind my roots in strategic and commercial thinking.

    Ideally, I would be in a role that intersects all my interests - the technical side of technology, the strategic and commercial side of running a business, and healthcare (because in the end, your health is all that matters).

    I think now is a good time for a breather!

    I live on the fun-side of life

    Humour is the best cure in life. There's almost nothing better than a good bant or outburst of laughter!

    I also love to travel and explore new areas. Love going to that one country, but sick of visiting the same city? Take a road-trip to the country-side and fall in love again!

    I try to live like a minimalist

    One of the reasons I love Japanese design is because of its simplicity and minimalism. Just look up the art of 'Kintsugi'. Simply beautiful.

    The universe is chaotic enough so every bit of effort to be more minimalist and orderly helps. I try to live by this at home and at work, so now you know why I keep things tidy!

    Other Stuff

    I do appreciate you taking the time to learn more about me. Other than the above, I also love food and quality time with friends and family. I have also started to really get into coffee, thanks to a few coffee-afficionado friends of mine...

    If you want to grab a (good) coffee and have a chat, get in touch!
